Non Drug Management of Irritable Bowel Syndrome

  • Avoid foods rich in sugars, soda, caffeine, alcohol
  • Eat whole meals, natural foods, fruits and vegetables
  • Try Low FODMAP diet. It's been shown to improve symptoms.Foods that have high FODMAP (F- fermentable; O- oligosaccharides; D- disaccharides; M-monosaccharides; A-and; P- polyols) include the following (should be avoided): wheat, barley, onions, leek, garlic, peas, cashews, milk, custard, ice-cream, yoghurt, apples, pears, mangoes, cherries, watermelons, honey, asparagus, apricots, cauliflower, artificially sweetened chewing gum)
  • Eat deliberately and slowly
  • Some people find benefits from avoiding milk and gluten products
  • Destress yourself via meditation, massage and exercise
  • Try probiotics ie good bacteria
  • Peppermint oil
  • Acupuncture
  • Increase physical activity and try eliminating gluten and or lactose
  • Talk to a health professional
