Diabetes and Diet

The plans have variable ratio of fats, proteins and carhohydrates

Generally, the following are recommended:


Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates should have low glycaemic loads and come from vegetables.

Fat and proteins should come from plant sources.

NB.

Glycaemic index is a scientific team used to measure the impact of carbohydrate on blood sugar.

Foods with low index raise blood sugar in modest terms and are thus better choices for patients with diabetes mellitus.

Complex cardohydrates have low glycaecmic indices. These have in addition; fibre, vitamins and smaller amounts of proteins and fats. The latter tend to slow down the absorption of glucose and keep blood sugar levels in a less erratic state.

Examples of the foods in this category include:

  • Brown rice
  • Whole wheat
  • Vegetables
  • Fruits
  • Beans
  • Lentils
  • Oatmeal
  • Potatoes
  • Corn
  • Root vegetables

High glycaemic index foods that should not be included the diet of a diabetic patient incude:

  • Sugar
  • White pasta
  • Watermelon
  • Pastries
  • Fruit juice
  • Pineapples
  • Sweets
  • Cookies
  • Flour
  • White bread
  • White pasta

Fats and oils

Although fats do not directly influence blood sugar, they are useful in slowing down the absorption of carbohydrates. Additional benefits of fats and oils include:

  • Fermented dairy products decrease the risk of cardiovascular disease
  • Plant fats lower cardiovascular diseases. Sources of these include olive oil, seeds, avocado and nuts
  • Proportionately smaller amount of fat provides sateity and this works to advantage in reducing the tendency to overeat

Proteins

Proteins are a steady source of energy and it does provide this without making the blood sugar to be erratic. They keep the blood sugar stable, provide sateity and contribute to the metabolism of the body.

Examples of good sources of protein include:

  • Legumes such as beans, pea nuts
  • Eggs
  • Seafoods
  • Unprocessed dairy products
  • Lean parts of meat such as chicken and turkey

A typical diet plan for a patient with diabetes should have a lot of vegetables and with limited amounts of processed sugars and red meat

Diabetes and Weight

Weight gain and weight problem is not an uncommon accompaniment of diabetes mellitus. The loss of even a small amount of weight can help a diabetic patient to produce and utilise insulin more effectively (this applies to type 2 diabetes mellitus).

Strategies to lose weight in diabetes include:

  • Eat lower calories
  • Bariatric surgery
  • Exercise: The recommended amount of exercise is 30 minutes per day most days of the week
  • Use of weight loss medications
