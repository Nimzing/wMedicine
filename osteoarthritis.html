Non Drug Management of Osteoarthritis

Non pharmacological management of osteoarthritis should be tried first, failure of which should provoke on-demand use of analgesics. A few of these modalities are advised below:

Exercise

The value of exercise in the control of pain associated with osteoarthritis cannot be overemphasised. It is estimated to be comparable to the magnitude of response when non steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs such as Ibuprofen, are used. Aerobic and strengthening exercises have been shown to help.

Weight loss

Among the causal factors of osteoarthritis is overweight and obesity. Evidence amounts to show that with a 10% in weight loss either by drugs or diet, there is an associated 50% reduction in pain scores from osteoarthritis in obese patients.

Walking aids/knee braces

Knee braces (if osteoarthritis of the knees) and or walking aids (hip joint involvement) would help to reduce pressures in inflamed joints and thereby aumenting the effect of analgesics.
