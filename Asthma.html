Non Pharmacological Management of Asthma

Identify Triggers

Indoor allergens including dust mites, animal danders, molds, mice, cockroaches are recognised triggers. Appropriate actions may require removal of rugs and or clearing of piles that encourage the existence of these triggers.

Avoid Inhalational irritants

Avoid the following and or avoid out-door activities that are associated with increased exposure to the following:

  • Tobacco
  • Smoke from burning coals and fireplaces
  • Strong perfumes
  • Odors
  • Chlorine-based cleaning products
  • Air pollutants

Control co-morbid conditions

It is not unusual to have asthma co-existing with other medical conditions; controlling of which would decrease the emotional tension that has the potential to worsen/exacerbate asthma. Some of these conditions include:

  • COPD
  • Gastro-oesophageal reflux disease
  • Obesity
  • Sleep apnoea
  • Chronic depression

Influenza

Influenza presents seasonally and has the potential to exacerbate asthma. There is vaccine available for this and it is advisable to have it when the season comes. This way, the care of your asthma is optimised.
